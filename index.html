The ecosystem

Creometry is a socio-technical ecosystem that applies commoning1 practices to computing infrastructure (aka. the cloud2).

In the spirit of commoning, we believe in sharing resources and sharing rewards.

Contributors to the Creometry ecosystem earn rewards3 based on course, code and compute resource contributions.

In summary, Creometry is composed of:

  • Academy: a technology training ground for developers and teams.
  • Vessel: a space where developers meet opportunities and contribute to project initiatives.
  • Cloud Platform: A network of kubernetes clusters capable of handling diverse workloads.

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to your question. If still curious? Then Drop us a message!

  • What is commoning?

    Commoning is a practice of collaborating and sharing to meet everyday needs and achieve well-being, of individuals, communities and lived-in environments. Learn more

  • How does creometry apply commoning to cloud infrastructure?

    “The cloud is just someone else's computers”, racked in multiple data centers. Creometry's cloud infrastructure is a network of computers owned by individuals who volunteerily join clusters where compute resources are pooled to host application workloads.

  • Is Creometry a "blockchain" network?

    While creometry incentives are inspired by virtual-currency mining, creometry's network does not store decentralized ledgers nor facilitates any form of speculative financial schemes such as "ICO", "IEO", "STO", etc. The ecosystem will only support a unique "crypto-token" designed specific functions which will be announced later.

  • What kind of application workloads can I deploy on creometry's cloud platform?

    Containers! Creometry allows you to run containerized workloads on kubernetes clusters.

  • Who is Creometry for?

    Creometry is built for underserved communities who have limited access to public clouds, mainly due to currency exchange controls, which limits international payments including payments to major cloud providers. Creometry is also a good fit for individuals, startups, researchers, or even enterprises interested in a low-cost Dev/Staging environment, or looking to leverage the incentive system to onboard early adopters of a new feature/application.

  • Should I be concerned about the security of my applications running on untrusted hosts?

    Creometry gives developers the option to deploy confidential containers, which isolate workloads and encrypts ALL network traffic and data. Although the number of undiscovered vultnerabilities is manageably small, you should avoid running critical workloads or compute sensitive data on Creometry's network.
